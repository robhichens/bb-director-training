## Learning Objectives

By the end of this section, you will be able to:
- Identify which incidents require immediate VDSS notification vs. internal handling
- Execute the mandatory reporter process correctly and without hesitation
- Lead your staff through a medical emergency before EMS arrives
- Implement lockdown or active-threat procedures
- Document every incident in a way that protects the center and the children

## The Director's Rule in Any Emergency

**You are the decision-maker. Not the lead teacher. Not the parent. You.**

When something goes wrong, staff will look to you. Even if you don't know exactly what to do yet, your calm and decisive presence is the first intervention. Take a breath. Assess. Act. Document.

## 1. Child Injury — Serious Incident Reporting

### What Counts as a "Serious Incident"

Virginia licensing requires you to notify VDSS **by the end of the business day** any time:

- A child requires emergency medical treatment (ER visit, ambulance called)
- A child sustains a head injury of any kind
- A child is unaccounted for (missing child — even briefly)
- A child dies while in care
- A child is the victim of suspected abuse or neglect **while in your care**
- Any incident that results in police response

> **When in doubt, report it.** An unreported incident that later surfaces is far more damaging than an over-reported one.

### Serious Incident Response — Step by Step

1. **Ensure the child is safe and receiving care.** Get your first aid trained staff or call 911 immediately if needed.
2. **Call 911 if there is any doubt about the child's condition.** Do not wait to see if it gets better.
3. **Notify the parent immediately.** Do not delay parent notification to "assess the situation first."
4. **Separate the involved staff and any witnesses.** Do not let them talk to each other before you take statements.
5. **Document independently.** Each witness writes their own account before comparing notes.
6. **Call Chrystal King (VDSS licensing):** 804-297-4469 — by end of business day.
7. **Call ownership (KP).** Do not let ownership hear about a serious incident from anyone other than you.
8. **Complete the Injury Report form** and place in the licensing binder.
9. **Follow up in writing** with the parent within 24 hours.

### What NOT to Do

- Do not admit fault or speculate about cause before facts are known
- Do not delete, edit, or rearrange Tadpoles documentation after an incident
- Do not give statements to attorneys or insurance adjusters without ownership present
- Do not discuss the incident with other families

## 2. Mandatory Reporter Obligations

**Every person who works at Bright Beginnings is a mandatory reporter under Virginia law.** This means you are legally required to report reasonable suspicion of child abuse or neglect. You do not need proof. You need reasonable suspicion.

### What Triggers a Report

- Unexplained bruising, burns, welts, or injuries inconsistent with the child's developmental stage
- A child discloses abuse or neglect to you or a staff member
- Significant behavioral changes suggesting trauma
- A parent or caregiver appears intoxicated or impaired at pickup
- A child appears consistently malnourished, dirty, or poorly cared for

### How to Report

**Virginia Child Abuse and Neglect Hotline: 1-800-552-7096** (24 hours)

1. Call the hotline immediately — do not wait until end of day.
2. Provide: child's name, age, address if known, description of what you observed or were told.
3. You will receive a report confirmation number — write it down.
4. Notify ownership (KP) after the call is made — not before.
5. Document in writing what you observed and when, who reported it, and the hotline confirmation number.

### If a Child Discloses to a Staff Member

- The staff member should **listen without interrupting or asking leading questions**
- Say: *"I hear you. You did the right thing by telling me. I'm going to make sure you're safe."*
- Stop the conversation — do not investigate further
- Come directly to you (the director) immediately
- That staff member documents exactly what the child said, word for word, as soon as possible

> **Your job is to report — not to investigate.** VDSS and law enforcement investigate. You protect the child and document what you know.

### Your Own Staff

If the suspected abuse happened in your building, at the hands of a staff member:

1. Remove the staff member from all child contact immediately (send home or reassign to non-child-contact duties)
2. Do not confront or interrogate the staff member
3. Call the hotline
4. Call ownership
5. Do not allow the staff member to return until cleared by VDSS and ownership

## 3. Medical Emergency in the Classroom

### Life-Threatening Emergencies — Call 911 First

**Never delay calling 911 to call a parent or consult with a supervisor.** Signs that require an immediate 911 call:

- Child is unresponsive or unconscious
- Child is not breathing or struggling to breathe
- Child is having a seizure (first seizure, or seizure lasting more than 5 minutes)
- Child has a severe allergic reaction (throat swelling, difficulty breathing, hives spreading)
- Child has ingested a substance (medication, cleaning product, unknown item)
- Significant bleeding that cannot be controlled
- Head injury with loss of consciousness, vomiting, or confusion

### While Waiting for EMS

1. **One staff member stays with the child at all times** — do not leave a sick or injured child alone
2. **Clear other children from the area** — move to another room or outdoor space; do not let other children witness a medical emergency
3. **Retrieve the child's Emergency Card and Medication Authorization form** — EMS will ask for this
4. **Send a staff member to the parking lot** to flag down the ambulance
5. **You or a trusted staff member accompanies the child to the hospital if a parent cannot be reached** — never let a child go to the ER alone

### Allergic Reactions / EpiPen

- **EpiPen is in the medicine box** — the only items permitted in the medicine box are inhalers and EpiPens
- If a child's EpiPen is administered, **call 911 immediately afterward** — EpiPen buys time, it does not resolve the reaction
- Staff authorized to administer an EpiPen must have current training on file

### Seizures

- **Do not restrain the child.** Move objects away from them.
- **Time the seizure.** If it lasts more than 5 minutes, call 911 immediately.
- **Do not put anything in the child's mouth.**
- Turn the child gently onto their side after the seizure stops to prevent choking.
- A child who has a first-time seizure at your center goes to the ER — no exceptions.

### Choking

All lead teachers must be current on infant/child CPR and first aid. As director, you should be too. If a child is actively choking:

- For children over 1 year: Heimlich maneuver
- For infants: 5 back blows + 5 chest thrusts (never abdominal thrusts on infants)
- If the child becomes unconscious: begin CPR and call 911

## 4. Active Threat / Lockdown

### Lockdown Triggers

Initiate lockdown when:
- Law enforcement requests it
- An unauthorized or threatening person enters or attempts to enter the building
- There is a threat in the immediate surrounding area (active police situation, armed individual reported nearby)
- Your gut tells you something is very wrong — trust it

### Lockdown Procedure

1. **Announce lockdown clearly** — use whatever in-building communication system you have (intercom, GroupMe, in-person if needed)
2. **All staff bring children inside immediately** — outdoor play stops
3. **Lock all exterior doors** — Brivo or manual
4. **Each classroom:** lock the door, cover the door window, move children away from windows and doors to an interior wall
5. **Lights off, silence** — no movement near windows or doors
6. **Do NOT open the door for anyone** until law enforcement gives all-clear in person — not over the phone
7. **Call 911** if there is an immediate threat inside the building
8. **Account for all children and staff** — text or GroupMe headcount to you as director

### Reunification After Lockdown

- Children are only released to authorized individuals on their emergency contact card
- Government-issued ID required at pickup — no exceptions, even for parents you recognize
- Document every child released, to whom, and at what time
- If law enforcement is present, follow their direction on reunification

### Fire Evacuation vs. Lockdown

| Situation | Response |
|---|---|
| Fire/smoke | Evacuate — get everyone out |
| External threat | Lockdown — keep everyone in |
| Both simultaneously | Follow law enforcement direction |

> A fire alarm during a reported active shooter situation may be used to force people outside. Use judgment. Call 911 and follow their guidance.

## 5. Missing Child

A missing child is one of the most serious incidents a childcare center can face. Minutes matter.

### Immediate Steps

1. **Search the building and grounds immediately** — bathrooms, closets, storage areas, outdoor perimeter
2. **Account for all other children** — do not pull every staff member from supervision
3. **If not found within 3–5 minutes: call 911.** Do not wait longer.
4. **Call the parent** — simultaneously with or immediately after calling 911
5. **Call Chrystal King (VDSS): 804-297-4469**
6. **Lock down the building** — no one leaves until the child is found

### Prevention

- Attendance is taken every 30 minutes during transitions
- Door alarm audible any time an exterior door opens
- No child exits the building without a staff member — ever
- Outdoor play headcounts before going out and before coming in

## 6. Documentation After Any Emergency

Within 24 hours of any emergency, you need:

- **Written incident report** — what happened, when, who was involved, what action was taken, outcome
- **Witness statements** — each staff member involved writes their own account independently
- **Parent communication log** — who you called, when, what was said
- **Any regulatory notifications** — VDSS confirmation numbers, hotline report numbers
- **Follow-up plan** — what changes, if any, are being made as a result

File all documentation in the licensing binder. Send a copy to ownership. Retain for a minimum of 5 years.

> **If it isn't documented, it didn't happen.** This protects the children, your staff, and the center.
