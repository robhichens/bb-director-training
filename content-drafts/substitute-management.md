# Substitute & Coverage Management
*Draft content — Module 3 addition (Section 3.7)*

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this section, you will be able to:
- Build and maintain an active substitute pool
- Correctly onboard a sub before they ever set foot in a classroom
- Execute a callout response procedure at 6 AM without panic
- Manage a coverage crisis across multiple classrooms simultaneously
- Keep your sub pool engaged so they actually answer when you call

---

## Why This Matters

Staff callouts are the single most disruptive daily event a director faces. One absence at the wrong ratio puts you out of compliance. Two absences and you're pulling yourself off the floor or combining classrooms.

A director with a strong sub pool handles a callout in 15 minutes. A director without one spends their morning in crisis mode — and the afternoon dealing with whatever fell apart because they were on the phone.

**Build the pool before you need it.** You cannot recruit subs during an emergency.

---

## 1. Building Your Sub Pool

### Target Size

You need a minimum of **6–8 active subs** on your list at any given time. "Active" means they've worked in the last 60 days and you have a current phone number for them.

Aim for coverage across all age groups — an infant-qualified sub is worth more than a general sub on a day your infant room teacher calls out.

### Where to Find Subs

- **Former staff** — ex-employees who left on good terms are your best source. They already know the children, the routines, and the building.
- **Local ECE college programs** — Piedmont Virginia Community College, other local programs. Students need practicum hours; you need coverage. Reach out to department heads directly.
- **Parents with ECE backgrounds** — occasionally a parent has a CDA or ECE degree and isn't working full-time. They already love the center.
- **Retired teachers** — elementary teachers often want occasional part-time work in retirement
- **Your current staff's networks** — ask A and B staff members if they know anyone qualified who wants occasional work

### Qualifications Required Before Any Sub Works

Every sub must have the following on file **before** their first shift. No exceptions.

| Document | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Fingerprints / background check | **Cannot be in the building without this.** Period. |
| TB test | Current (within 2 years) |
| CPR/First Aid | Current certification on file |
| VA licensing orientation | Must complete state-required orientation hours |
| Signed confidentiality agreement | On file with you |
| Emergency contact | On file |
| Copy of ID | On file |

> **The fingerprint rule is absolute.** It doesn't matter if someone is a retired teacher with 30 years of experience. If their fingerprints aren't cleared, they don't enter the building.

## 2. Onboarding a New Sub

Before their first shift, every sub gets an orientation that covers:

**Building basics (15–20 minutes, in person):**
- Walk the building — classrooms, bathrooms, kitchen, exits
- Where the first aid kits are (FL: 3, MC+CR: 2)
- Emergency evacuation routes and meeting spot
- Who to contact in an emergency (you — not another teacher)
- Attendance procedures — they must be able to do a headcount

**Classroom basics:**
- The daily schedule for the age group they'll typically cover
- Tadpoles — they don't need to post, but they need to know it exists
- Diapering procedure (if covering infant/toddler)
- Nap setup — blinds UP, lights ON
- What they can and cannot do without a lead teacher's approval (no field trips, no medication administration unless specifically trained and authorized)

**The non-negotiables:**
- No cell phone use around children — locked away or in pocket on silent
- They are always supervised by or accessible to a lead teacher for their first three shifts
- If a situation arises they're unsure about, they find you — immediately

## 3. The Callout Response Protocol

### When You Get the Call

Callouts happen most often between 5:30–7:30 AM. When your phone rings at 6:15 AM with "I can't make it today," here's how you respond:

**Step 1: Confirm the details immediately**
- "Are you calling out for the full day or partial?"
- "Is this a sick day or personal day?" (matters for payroll)
- Do not ask "are you sure?" or make them feel guilty — it doesn't help

**Step 2: Assess the impact**
- Which classroom, which ratio, what time they were scheduled to start
- Do you fall out of ratio at open, or do you have a buffer window?
- Is there a second absence today you already knew about?

**Step 3: Work the coverage list (in order)**

1. **Float staff or part-time staff first** — anyone with flexibility in their schedule
2. **Sub list** — start with those who've worked recently and are already familiar with the classroom
3. **Staff who might want extra hours** — text, don't call, at 6 AM unless urgent
4. **Yourself** — you cover when no one else can, but this is your last resort, not your first
5. **Classroom combination** — only after all else fails, and only if ratios allow

**Step 4: Confirm and document**
- Confirm coverage in writing (GroupMe or text)
- Log the callout in ProCare for payroll accuracy
- Note in your DOR if coverage was difficult or required you to alter ratios

### Coverage Priority Order

| Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 callout, float available | Float covers, subs on standby |
| 1 callout, no float | Call sub list |
| 2 callouts same day | Sub list + consider classroom combination |
| 3+ callouts | Classroom combination + call ownership |
| No sub available, out of ratio | You cover personally; notify ownership immediately |

> **You cannot open a classroom out of ratio.** If you cannot achieve legal ratio, that classroom does not operate.

### If You Cannot Find Coverage

If you've exhausted your list and cannot cover:
1. Call ownership before 7:30 AM — before families start arriving
2. Ownership may authorize combining classrooms, adjusting schedules, or bringing in outside support
3. Do not tell families "we're short-staffed today" — simply say "Ms. [name] is out today and Ms. [name] is covering"
4. Document the callout, your coverage attempts, and the outcome in your DOR

## 4. Keeping Your Sub Pool Active

The biggest mistake directors make is only contacting subs when they need them. By then, good subs have found other commitments.

**Monthly maintenance:**
- Text or call every sub who hasn't worked in 30 days — just a quick check-in: "Hey, we have some openings coming up, are you still available?"
- Offer at least one shift per month to each active sub so they stay familiar with the center
- Remove subs who haven't responded in 60 days from your active list

**Make it easy to say yes:**
- Give subs as much advance notice as possible — last-minute is sometimes unavoidable, but planned absences (doctor appointments, personal days) should have coverage arranged days in advance
- Pay subs promptly and correctly — a sub who has ever had a paycheck issue will deprioritize your calls
- Treat subs well while they're in the building — a sub who feels valued comes back; one who feels like a warm body doesn't

**Pipeline thinking:**
- Always be looking for the next sub, even when you're fully covered
- Any time you meet a qualified person (at a community event, through a parent, through a staff referral), get their contact information and follow up

## 5. Sub Pay Rates

*Confirm current rates with ownership — these are guidelines.*

| Role | Rate |
|---|---|
| General sub (preschool/toddler) | Hourly, confirm with KP |
| Infant-qualified sub | Slight premium — confirm with KP |
| Sub who steps into lead teacher role | Lead teacher rate for that shift |

Subs are paid through the same payroll process as regular staff. They must clock in and out accurately in ProCare.

## 6. Planned Absence Coverage

Not every absence is a surprise. Staff will request:
- Vacation days (should have at least 2 weeks notice)
- Scheduled medical appointments
- Personal days per their agreement

For **any planned absence:**
- Coverage must be confirmed in writing before the day off is approved
- The staff member requesting the day off does not find their own coverage — that is your job
- Planned absences go into the weekly scheduling review (Tuesday — see Module 2)

> Build the habit: every Thursday when you submit the Director Packet, look two weeks ahead for any scheduled absences and confirm coverage exists. A callout at 6 AM is a crisis. A known absence on Thursday is just scheduling.

## Director Self-Coverage: The Last Resort Rule

You will sometimes cover a classroom yourself. This is a reality of the job.

But every minute you're covering a classroom is a minute you're not directing the center. Phones go unanswered. Tours get missed. Incidents don't get managed. Administrative tasks pile up.

**Rules for director self-coverage:**
- It's a last resort, not a first response
- Maximum half a day before you escalate to ownership for support
- While covering, identify a responsible lead teacher to serve as your building eyes
- Document that you covered and why in your DOR — ownership needs visibility into coverage gaps
